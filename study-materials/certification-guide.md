## Exam Details — Level by Level

### Goethe A1 — Start Deutsch 1
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Format | Lesen (Reading) + Hören (Listening) + Schreiben (Writing) + Sprechen (Speaking) |
| Total time | ~65 minutes |
| Speaking format | Introduce yourself + respond to prompts (partnered) |
| Writing task | Fill in a form + write 3 short sentences |
| Pass mark | 60% in each component |
| When to take | After 3–4 months of study |
| Practical use | Spouse/family visa to Germany |

**Top tips for A1:**
- Memorize self-introduction completely (name, age, country, job, hobbies)
- Practice filling German forms (name, address, date of birth format: TT.MM.JJJJ)
- The listening section uses everyday situations: at the café, at the train station

### Goethe B1 — Zertifikat Deutsch
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Format | Lesen + Hören + Schreiben + Sprechen |
| Duration | ~3 hours |
| Speaking | Discuss a topic with a partner + plan something together |
| Writing | Email (80–100 words) or short essay |
| Pass mark | 60% per component |
| When to take | 12–14 months in |
| Why it matters | Required for German citizenship + permanent residency |

**Top tips for B1:**
- Writing task: practice formal email format (Sehr geehrte/r..., Mit freundlichen Grüßen)
- Speaking: you are paired with another test-taker — lead the conversation, don't wait
- Reading: read DW articles daily in the 2 months before the exam

### Goethe B2
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Format | Lesen + Hören + Schreiben + Sprechen |
| Duration | ~3.5 hours |
| Writing | Argumentative essay (~200 words) |
| Speaking | Presentation + discussion on a complex topic |
| Pass mark | 60% per component |
| When to take | ~22 months in |
| Why it matters | Work authorization, most employer requirements, some professions |

**Top tips for B2:**
- Essay structure: Introduction → Argument 1 → Argument 2 → Counter-argument → Conclusion
- Use connectors: "Einerseits...andererseits", "Außerdem", "Dennoch", "Zusammenfassend"
- Speaking: express and defend your opinion clearly — "Meiner Meinung nach..."

## Alternative Exams (When C1+ Is Needed)

### TestDaF — Test Deutsch als Fremdsprache
- **Purpose:** University admission in Germany
- **Levels:** TDN 3, 4, 5 (C1 = TDN 4, C2-adjacent = TDN 5)
- **Format:** Lesen + Hören + Schreiben + Sprechen
- **Note:** More academic in tone than Goethe
- **Website:** testdaf.de

### telc Deutsch
- **Levels:** A1–C1 (C2 not offered by telc in same format)
- **Advantage:** More test centers in India, slightly easier registration
- **B2 variant:** telc Deutsch B2 Beruf — specifically for professional contexts
- **Website:** telc.net

### DSH — Deutsche Sprachprüfung für den Hochschulzugang
- **Purpose:** University admission only
- **Taken at:** German university directly (not a global exam center)
- **Level:** DSH-2 ≈ B2/C1, DSH-3 ≈ C1
- **When relevant:** If you apply to a German university

## Exam Preparation Timeline (Per Level)

### 2 Months Before the Exam
- [ ] Download official sample papers from goethe.de
- [ ] Take 1 full mock exam under timed conditions
- [ ] Identify your weakest component (reading/listening/writing/speaking)

### 1 Month Before
- [ ] Do 2 full mock exams
- [ ] Focus 50% of study time on weakest component
- [ ] Practice writing 2 essays per week
- [ ] Book 2 speaking sessions with an iTalki tutor

### 2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Final vocabulary review (Anki burst)
- [ ] Do 1 mock exam per week — focus on timing
- [ ] No new grammar topics — consolidate existing knowledge

### Exam Day
- Arrive 30 min early
- Bring: passport/ID, registration confirmation, 2 pens
- Speaking section: it's OK to use filler phrases — "Also...", "Ich meine...", "Genau"
- Writing: structure first, then write (outline takes 3 min, saves 15)

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Grade | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 85–100% | Sehr gut | Excellent |
| 75–84% | Gut | Good |
| 60–74% | Befriedigend | Pass |
| Below 60% | Nicht bestanden | Fail (retake needed) |

You must pass each component separately — a high score in one cannot compensate for a fail in another.
